Expressions

A combination of constants and variables connected by +, -,* or ÷ signs is known as an algebraic expression. For example: 3x2 - 5, x3 - 2xy and others.

KEY TERMS IN EXPRESSIONS MEANING
TERM Several parts of an algebraic expression seperated by + or - signs are known as terms.
Coefficient A coefficient is the numerical part of a term, including the sign that precedes it (+ or –).
Important: Every term has a coefficient. When a term appears to have no coefficient, its coefficient is either 1 or –1, depending on the sign.
For example: • The term 3x has a coefficient of 3, • The term –9x has a coefficient of –9, • The term –x has a coefficient of –1.
Variable A variable is any letter that stands for a number. The most commonly used letters are x and y, but you can use any letter.
Constant A constant is a number without a variable. For example: The equation 6m + 7 = –m has one constant, the number 7.
Polynomials An expression in which the variables involved have only NON NEGATIVE integer powers.
Like Terms Terms having the same literal coefficients are called like terms. Ex: 3x3- 5x3
Unlike Terms Terms having different literal coefficients are called unlike terms. Ex: -3x2- 5x3
Degree of the Polynomial The highest exponent of the variable in a polynomial of 1 variable is called the degree of the polynomial. Ex: x⁴ - 5x² - x + 7 is a polynomial of degree 4

Simplification of an expression

• Find the like terms and then add or subtract them based on their co-effecient.
• Ensure an expression has no 2 seperate like terms.
• Such expression is the simplified expresson.

Addition and Subtraction of polynomials.

Addition: The sum of two or more polynomials can be obtained by arranging the terms and then adding the numerical co-effecients of like terms.
Subtraction: The difference of two or more polynomials can be obtained by arranging the terms and then subtracting the numerical co-effecients of like terms.

EQUATIONS

An open sentence containing the equality sign is an equation. In other words, an equation is a sentence in which there is an equals to sign between 2 algebraic expressions.

Simple and Linear Equations

An equation in which the highest index of the unkown present is one, is a linear equation.
A linear equation which has only onw unkown/variable is a simple equation.

How to solve and equation

Step 1: Always ensure that the unkown quantities are on the LHS and the known quantities or constants on the RHS side.
Step 2: Add all the terms containing the unkowns on the LHS and all the knows on the RHS side
Step 3: Divide bith sides of the equation by the coefficient of the unkown.

Triangles

A three-sided simple closed figure is a triangle. A triangle has three sides and has 3 angles. The sum of angles in a triangle is 180°. There are 7 types of triangle which can be segregated based on Sides and Angles.

  1. Scalene triangle- A triangle in which no two side is equal. Based on SIDES
  2. Isosceles triangle- A triangle in which any two sides are equal. Based on SIDES
  3. Equilateral triangle- A triangle in which all three sides are equal. Based on SIDES
  1. Acute-angled triangle- A triangle in which all the three angles are less than 90°. Based on ANGLES
  2. Right-angled triangle- A triangle in which one angle is 90°. Based on ANGLES
  3. Obtuse-angled triangle- A triangle in which one angle is more than 180°. Based on ANGLES
  4. Isosceles right-angled triangle- A triangle in which two sides are equal and one angle is 90°. Based on ANGLES

Pythagoras Therom

The Pythagoras Thearom is a very important theorm which states that- a2 + b2 = c2. This formula works for right angled triangles. To find any one side of the triangle, this equation is used. The a² represents the vertical line in the right triangle, the b² represents the horizontal line in the right triangle and the c² represents the hypotenuse or the slanting line of the right triangle.
An example of pythogoros theorm is:
A triangle has a vertical length of 6cm and a horizontal length of 8cm. What is the size of the Hypotenuse(Slanting side)?
First in the formula we do the square of the horizontal and the vertical. Which is- 6x6 = 36cm and 8x8 = 64cm. The square of the length of the hypotenuse is 36 + 64 = 100cm. The √100 = 10. So the length of the hypotenuse is 10cm. The hypotenuse is ALWAYS longer the other 2 side of the triangle.



Congruency of TRIANGLES

The three andlges of a triangle determine it's shape and it's three sides determine it's size. If the three angles and the three sides of triangle are respectively equal to the corresponding angles and sides of another triangle, the 2 triangles are said to be congruent. How every, to conclude that the triangles are congruent, it is not necessary that all the six corresponding elements of the two triangles are given to be congruent.

Based on the study and experiments, the following results can be used to establish the congruence angles of the second triangle. Below are the given properties which helps us to check if the triangles are congruent.

Property 1- SSS (Side, Side and Side)

The SSS Property, two triangles are congruent if the three sides of a triangle are equal to the corresponding sides of the other triangle.

In △ABC and △XYZ, if AB = XY, BC = YZ AND CA = XZ, then △ABC is congruent to △XYZ

Property 2- SAS (Side, Angle and Side)

By the side angle side congruence property, two triangles are congruent if the two sides and the included angle of one triangle are equal to the corresponding sides and the included angle of the other triangle, i.e, the third side and the other two angles of the first triangle are bound to be equal to the corresponding side and angles of the second triangle.

In △ABC and △XYZ, if AB = XY, BC = YZ AND ∠C = ∠Z, then △ABC is congruent to △XYZ

Property 3- ASA (Angle, Side and Angle)

By the angle side angle congruence property, two triangles are congruent if any two angles and the side included between them of one triangle are equal to the corresponding angles and the side included between them of one triangle are equal to the corresponding angles and the included side of the other triangle, i.e the third angle and the other two sides of the first triangle are bound to be equal to the corresponding angle and sides of the second triangle.

In △ABC and △XYZ, if AB = XY ∠B = ∠Y AND ∠A = ∠X, then △ABC is congruent to △XYZ

Property 4- RHS(Right, Hypotenuse and Side)

The Right Hypotenuse side property states that two right angled triangles are congruent if the hypotenuse and one side of one triangle are equal to the hypotenuse and the corresponding side of the other triangle, i.e, the third side and the other two angles of the first triangle are bound to be equal to the corresponding side and angles of the second triangle.

In △TSU and △FHG, if ST = HG ∠S = ∠H AND ∠U = ∠T, then △TSU is congruent to △FHG
