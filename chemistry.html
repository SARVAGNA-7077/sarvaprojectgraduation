Relavance of CHEMISTRY

Chemistry is important because everything you do is chemistry! Even your body is made of chemicals. Chemical reactions occur when you breathe, eat, or just sit there reading. All matter is made of chemicals, so the importance of chemistry is that it's the study of everything.
Food is made from chemicals. Many of the changes you observe in the world around you are caused by chemical reactions. Examples include leaves changing colors, cooking food and getting yourself clean. Knowing some chemistry can help you make day-to-day decisions that affect your life.

METALS AND NON METALS

PERIODIC TABLE-

Periodic table consists of Elements which can be grouped as METALS, NON METALS AND METALLOIDS. The rows of the periodic table are called periods while coloumns are called as gruops. There are a total of 7 Rows and 18 Coloumns. The elements in a coloumn have the same valency electrons. In a row, the elements are arranged in increasing atomic number. Atomic number is the number of protons present in the nucleus of an element. Valency electrons are the number of electrons in the outer most shell in the element.
Most of the elements in the periodic table are metals. There are 91 metals, 19 nonmetals and 7 mettaloids.

What are metals, non metals and metalloids?


The materials which do not posses the characteristics of metals are known as NON METALS. Ex- SULPHUR, PHOSPHURUS, CARBON AND OXYGEN.
The materials which posses the properties of both metals and non metals are known as METALLOIDS.Examples- Boron, Silicon, Germanium, Arsenic, Tellurium, Antimony and Polonium.

METALS

The materials that posses the characteristics of being hard, malleable, shiny, fusible and ductile, are called as METALS. Ex- IRON, ALLUMINIUM, GOLD AND COPPER.

Types of Metals

There are 5 types of metals. They are ALKALI METALS, ALKALI EARTH METALS, TRANSITION METALS, LANTHANIDES AND ACTINIDES.
Alkali metals react with water to produce heat, hydrogen gas, and the corresponding metal hydroxide.
Alkaline-earth metals comprise Group 2 of the periodic table. The elements are beryllium (Be), magnesium (Mg), calcium (Ca), strontium (Sr), barium (Ba), and radium (Ra).
Transition metals are the metals which often do not react much with other metals. They are less reactive compared to the other metals.
Lanthanides are the members of the 15 naturally occurring metallic chemical elements, whose atomic numbers fall between 57 (Lanthanum) and 71 (Lutetium). Lanthanides have similarities in their physical and chemical properties and have many industrial and scientific uses .
Actinides are elements with atomic numbers from 90 to 103 following element Actinium. They include naturally occurring elements of thorium, protactinium and uranium and eleven transuranic i.e., artificially produced by nuclear reactions. Nevertheless, all actinides are radioactive.
Alloys- Many metals that we use today are alloys. Alloys are metals that combine 2 or more elements. They could also be called metallic compounds. Alloys are stronger and harder than pure metals. Example Steel, Stainless steel (Fe + C), Brass(Cu + Zn) and Bronze (Cu + Sn).

About mettaloids-

The six commonly recognised metalloids are boron, silicon, germanium, arsenic, antimony, and tellurium. ... Typical metalloids have a metallic appearance, but they are brittle and only fair conductors of electricity. Chemically, they behave mostly as nonmetals. They can form alloys with metals. They have both the property of metals and non metals.

What is ACID AND BASES?

Acids are substances which furnishes H+ ions as the only positively charged ions upon dissolution in water.
Bases are substances which furnishes OH- ions as the only negativly charged ions upon dissolution in water. When there are more hydrogen ions, the substance is acidic. When there are more Hydroxide ions, the substance is basic.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Strong / Weak ACID and BASES

The acids and bases which undergo complete ionization when dissolved in water and furnish very large concentration of H+ ions or OH - ions, respectively are called strong acid and bases.
The acids and bases which ionizes partially when dissolved in water and furnish very less concentration of H+ ions or OH - ions, respectively are called weak acid and bases

Examples-
Weak Acids- • Acetic Acid - CH3COOH, • Carbonic Acid - H2CO3
Weak Bases- • Calcium Hydroxide - Ca(OH)2, • Alluminium Hydroxide - Al(OH)3

Examples-

pH Scale

pH Scale is a ruler that we can use to measure how acidic or basic a substance is. There are 14 different possible point values on the scale and each one represents a possible level of acidity or baseness
A substance is acidic if it has a pH level 0 through 7 where 0 is the most acidic.
A substance is basic if it has a pH of 7 through 14 where 14 is the most basic
If a substance has a pH of 7 it is neutral. Examples- Water (7), Blood (7.35 - 7.45)

Neutralization

The reaction between an acid and a base gives salt and water ansd is called Neutralization. In a Neutralization reaction, one H+Ion of acid is nuetralized by one OH-Ion of base. When all the H+ in the acidic solution are neutralized by same number of OH- ions of basic solution, it is called Complete Neutralization. During this process a point called Neutralization Point Is reached when all the H+ Ions are neutralized by the OH-ions. Further addition of a base or an acid changes the nature of the solution. Substances used to identify the neutralization point are called indicators.

indicators
