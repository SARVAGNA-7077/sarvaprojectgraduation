Democracy

Democracy is a form of government in which people exercise their right to vote by choosing their representatives for the state or a country.

Universal adult franchise

The major feature of democracy is the right to elect or vote our own representatives. The right to vote was not given to all the people in the past. Only men and the influential people were allowed to vote. The suffragate movement in the 19th and the 20th century ensured that women also get the right to vote. However in India, all citizens above the age of 18 irrespective of their caste, creed, religion and gender are given the right to vote. This is called the UNIVERSAL ADULT FRANCHISE



Election commission of India (ECI)

• This is a constitutional, independent to oversees free and fair elections throught the country.
• This was established on 25 January, 1950.

Composition

• The body is comprised of a chief election commissioners and 2 elections commissioners, appointed by the president.
• They have a tenure of 6 years or up to the age of 65 years, which ever is earlier.

Functions and powers of ECI

• Determine territories of the electoral constituencies.
• Recognition of political parties and allocates symbols
• Conduct and supervise elections and Byelections
• Advise president, governor on the question of disqualification of any member
• Scrutinize election expense accounts submitted by the contestants.

Electoral Process

• The election commission announces the dates of the elections and the code of conduct to be followed by all the parties
• The commission prepares the voters list, which contains the names of all the people who are eligible to vote, before the elections takes place.
• The commission issues electoral photo identity card to all the eligible voters and voting is done through a secret ballot.
• Finally, ECI announces the result of the elections and submits the list of the successful candidates.





Political parties

• A political party is an organization that has a common ideology and aims to form the government by competing with other parties for the votes of the citizen.
• Parties that have presence through out the country are called national parties. Ex- INC, BJP etc. While, Parties that have presence limited to a certain region or a state are called regional parties. Ex- TRS, TMC, AIADMK etc.
• Parties that gets majority of seats form government and known as ruling party. The other parties form the opposition.




Electronic Voting Machines (EVM)

EVMs are used to record the votes casted by the people. The names of the candidates and party symbols are shown on the voting machines. The voters just have to press the button aganist the name of the candidate of the people's choice. These machines are sealed after the voting ends.





Coalition government

The Lok Sabha consists of 545 members who are elected for a term of 5 years. In order to form the government, a political party must secure atleast 51% majority. When different political parties combine together, it is known as Coalition government. Such a government requires a lot of cooperation between the allies in order to ensure the stability of the government.

What is ECONOMICS ?

Economics is a social science that deals with the production, distribution and consumption of goods and services. It is all about money being made and money being spent. In this, we can learn about how people become rich or poor. The economy consists of buyers and sellers. Most of us are both buyers and sellers. We buy what we want and need for our lives and we sell our time and expertise to our employers.

There are times when a lot of people have more money than they need to survive. When this happens, the economy is doing well and the job market is good. There are times when many people don't have enough money to buy what they need. The economy is not doing good when this happens. In bad economy, people won’t spend too much money. They only buy the things they need and take care of what they already have as the prices of things go up.

Economic and Non economic activities

Economic Activity- An economic activity is an activity of providing, making, buying, or selling of commodities or services by people to satisfy their day-to-day needs of life. It can also include activities that deal with the manufacturing, distributing or utilizing of products or services.
In short, activities that involve money or the exchange of products or services are economic activities.
The three types of economic activities are as follows:

Activity How
Business Provides goods and services to satisfy human needs on a daily basis with the aim of earning profits.
Profession An occupation or a professional job that offers specialized services in return for professional charges.
Employment Contract between the company and the employee. Here, the employee performs duties for the company, and is paid (with wages or salary) in return.

Features of Economic Activities:

• Earning Motive
• Utility Creation
• Satisfaction
• Measurement

Non Economic Activity- A non-economic activity is an activity performed with the purpose of rendering services to others without any considerations of financial gains. Activities that are initiated for personal content or for meeting human sentiments are non-economic activities.
Some common examples of non-economic activities are the following:

Activity How
Free time activities They aim at acquiring pleasure during idle times. For example: painting, and singing, etc.
Family commitment activities These activities are dedicated and inspired by the institution of family. For example, a teacher offering education to own child at home, a doctor consulting a patient in his family, etc.
Cultural and religious activities These activities includes the ideas of happiness and mental satisfaction. For example, e.g. worshiping God, visiting the temple, etc.
Social welfare activities They are based on the passion for helping or sympathising towards others in the society. They include donations to the underprivileged, participation in relief camps to help the people affected by war, natural calamities, etc.

The features of Economic Activities:


• Self Motive
• Not measured money
• Self Satisfaction
• Social Obligation

Goods and Services

Goods and services are an essential part of an economy. These two terms are used in most of the important economic discussions. There are many products that a consumer purchases in order to fulfill their certain requirements. These products can be either in the form of goods or services. The purpose of both goods and services is to provide utility and satisfaction to the consumer.

GOODS- The meaning of goods can be expressed in terms of economics, as any item that provides utility and fulfills the needs of the consumer. Goods can be classified as durable and non-durable based on their durability. Durable goods last for a long time while non-durable goods perish sooner than durable goods.
Goods involve transfer of ownership from the seller once it is purchased by the consumer (buyer). There is a certain time period that is required for the production of goods. Goods due to their tangible nature have a proper structure, size and shape. They can be produced as per the market demand.

SERVICES- Services are the intangible and non physical part of the economy that cannot be touched. They are perishable in nature as they need to be provided at a moment when requested by the consumer. Service lacks a physical identity and cannot be owned but can only be utilised. For e.g, when having dinner at a restaurant you can avail the concierge services but you do not own the restaurant.
In other words, there is no transfer of ownership in services and unlike goods, services cannot be stored and utilised later. Also, services cannot be distinguished from the service provider.

Economic Agents

Economic agents are decision makers who can recognize the different factors that influence and motivate different economic groups. They are consumers, producers or influencers of capital markets and the economy at large.



There are four major types of economic agents
-Consumers: One who consumes a produced good or service. Eg: street shoppers, stock brokers
-Business Firms: Economic agents whose role is to transform factors of production (land,labour,capital and enterprenuer) into goods and services to sell. They can be public and private(Private sector enterprises are owned by a group of individuals or a single entity, while public sector enterprises are owned partially or completely by the government
-Government: An economic agent which provides rules for how firms and consumers should interact
-Banking sector: An economic agent that facilitating business transaction.

WHAT IS CIRCULAR FLOW OF INCOME?

The circular flow means the unending flow of production of goods and services, income, and expenditure in an economy. It shows the redistribution of income in a circular manner between the production units(Firms) and households. These are land, labour, capital, and entrepreneurship.
• Firms produce good s and services and sell them to the household.
• Households are the owners of factors of production and consumers of goods and services-

• The payment for the contribution made by fixed natural resources (called land) is known as rent.
• The payment for the contribution made by a human worker is known as wage.
• The payment for the contribution made by capital is known as interest.
• The payment for the contribution made by entrepreneurship is known as profit.

Sectors of Indian Economy

Indian economy is divided into various sectors based on different attibutes.

• Based on the proportion of popoluation engaged in different activities it is divided into three sectors- Primary, Secondary and Tertiary. This categorization represents a continuum of distance from the natural environment. The continuum starts with primary economic activity, which concerns itself with the utilization of raw materials from the earth, such as agriculture and mining. From there, the distance from natural resources increases as sectors become more detached from the processing of raw materials.

Few decades ago, Primary sector was the largest employer because not enough jobs were created in the Secondary and Tertiary sectors. However, lately Tertiary sector has emerged as the largest producing sector in India replacing the Primary sector.

• In terms of operations, the Indian economy is divided into organized and unorganized.
Organized Sector includes those enterprises or places of work where the terms of employment are regular and therefore, people have assured work.
Unorganised sector includes small and scattered units which are largely outside the control of the government. Farmers, small scale industry contribute majority into this sector. They need support by providing adequate facility for timely delivery of seeds, agricultural inputs, credit, storage facilities and marketing outlets and for procuring raw material and marketing of output.

• By ownership, it is divided into the public sector and private sector.
In the public sector, the government owns most of the assets and provides all the services.
In the private sector, private individuals or companies own assets and are responsible for delivery of services.
